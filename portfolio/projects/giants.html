GIANTS

TEAM FORTRESS 2

 
 

Giants is a rotationally symmetrical Capture the Flag map for Team Fortress 2 set in a region of snowy permafrost in Iceland. Development took place over two separate periods - the layout was rapidly iterated upon through April-March 2020, and was remade from scratch and artpassed over winter 2021-22. The map features custom round timer logic and emphasises a push-pull flow to prevent the stalemates typical of the mode.

Giants was originally developed for the Pick It Up! Contest, placing fourth (under a silly working name). Pretty good, considering how new I was to level design at that point!

 

Mid through whitebox iterations, and final.

 

Giants saw a large amount of iteration through development, though when it came to artpassing the project years later I decided to remake the layout from scratch - the old brushwork was pretty bad, making it hard to work with and a lot of layout decisions were difficult to untangle. With a fresh file I was able to take the general shape of the map - which from playtesting I knew worked pretty well - and remade it cleaner, while applying the knowledge I've gained since.

Mid saw a lot of changes through development. CtF mids are typically quite open, leading to a sort of no-man's-land - in Giants, mid takes more of a push-and-pull between both teams, while still being open enough to allow flanking players to sneak into the enemy base. This was a hard balance to strike! One of the biggest things I changed at mid when remaking the layout was removing the second floor on the flanking buildings, and pushing the staircase up to the battlements into a corner. The old layout had issues with hard mid holds, and these elements were the culprits. It was a lot clearer in hindsight!

 

Flag room and lobby through whitebox iterations, and finals.

 

A one-way dropdown into the flag room allows for surprise pushes - further reducing CtF stalemates by discouraging turtling tactics - but the windows overlooking it felt oppressive for the defending team, so it eventually ended up as a tight vent that allowed vision but not combat between the spaces unless the player in the vent commits to entering the flag room.

Lobby originally had a way up to the battlements. This layout element sucked, in hindsight, and was a symptom of how difficult the map as a whole became to work with. It's completely omitted from the remade layout, and is replaced with a stairwell that connects the layers of the base interior. Much easier to understand, and way better for player flow all round.

 

Custom flag logic entities and corresponding diagram.

 

Giants features a custom entity IO setup to properly handle a round timer (as by default, it will just stalemate immediately regardless of score if no team has hit the win threshold). It's available for others to download and use, too, and I made sure it was thoroughly documented.

The logic setup handles bonus time for when the losing team captures, and properly awards wins at timer runout based on flag statuses (dropped, carried, etc) instead of the terrible stock behaviour. There's a lot of fun tricks in here to work around TF2's lack of a full scripting language, like using comparator entities to store flag states.

More concept art; Initial sketches of areas, custom Flag model concept, colours drawover of a supercomputer model, and clarification on a part of the supercomputer model.

 

A fair amount of concept art was created for important elements of the map's visual identity. I worked closely alongside an artist throughout, and our combined efforts turned out nothing short of stunning!

 

The menu photo displayed during map load.

 

Giants is one of the best things I've made so far. It plays well, runs great, and brings a new basalty environment while keeping to TF2's art direction. To say I'm proud of not only my work, but the work of every contributor to the project, is a huge understatement. We did great!
